**Content/Summary/Key Takeaways**:

A stealth-mode AI founder went from 1 post to an intro with a $1B company in under 30 days.

The authority playbook the recognised SaaS names use, in 5 parts:

**1/ Fix your scoreboard before you write a single post.**

Most founders optimise for the highest engagement they can get.

The viral post, the big like count, the comment section going off.

The names ask the opposite question: what is the lowest-engagement post I can still profit from?

A post with a dozen likes that books a 5-figure deal beats a viral post that books nothing.

They know what a booked call is worth, so they know how much quiet content they can afford to run.

The benchmark to hit:

→ Buyers are saving your posts

→ Buyers visiting your profile

→ Buyers landing in your DMs

**2/ Build with blocks instead of guessing.**

Most founders write 20 random thoughts and hope something lands.

The winners treat every post as a building block: angle, persona, format, hook, funnel.

We have shipped 3,000+ posts for our founders, and none of it came from 3,000 random ideas.

It came from finding a winning block and changing 1 variable at a time.

A winning text post becomes a carousel, then a video, then the opening line of an outbound DM.

**3/ Let your hook do the targeting.**

On LinkedIn, there is no ad set.

Your hook calls out 1 specific person in line 1, whether that is the seed-stage AI founder buried in investor updates or the RevOps leader who inherited a broken pipeline.

When the right person sees themselves, they stop.

Everyone else scrolls, which is what you want.

Borrow trust too.

Feature a recognised customer, post a clip with a known operator, or tag a credible name.

**4/ Match the funnel to the post.**

The biggest mistake is sending every reader to the same book-a-call link. Match the next step to where the reader is:

→ Problem aware → A lead magnet or teardown that names the problem

→ Solution aware → A case study that shows your approach wins

→ Product aware → Straight to the booking form

**5/ Build the angle leaderboard.**

Use 1 naming convention across everything: PersonaAngleFormatHook.

Tag every post that way and pull it into a leaderboard.

Rank by angle and trigger, not likes.

The winners tell you what to write next.

The losers stop wasting your time. 1 winning angle becomes 10 posts.

10 posts become the reason a buyer trusts you before the first call.
