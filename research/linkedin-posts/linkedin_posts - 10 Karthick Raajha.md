**Content/Summary/Key Takeaways**:

ABM and traditional marketing are often compared as if one has to replace the other.

I don’t think that is the right way to look at it.

Both have a role. The difference is in what each one is built to do.

Traditional marketing helps B2B SaaS teams build reach, awareness, inbound demand, and visibility across a wider market.

ABM helps when the goal is more specific: identify high-value accounts, align sales and marketing around them, and create more relevant engagement with the people involved in the buying decision.

That distinction matters more in 2026.

Buying committees are larger. Sales cycles are longer. Marketing spend is under more scrutiny.

And teams are being asked to prove pipeline quality, not just campaign activity.

So the question is not “Should we do ABM or traditional marketing?”

The better question is:

 What kind of growth motion are we trying to build?

If the goal is broad awareness, traditional marketing still does an important job.

If the goal is to influence and convert high-value accounts, ABM gives the strategy more focus.

For many SaaS teams, the opportunity is in knowing where each approach fits and how both can work together.
