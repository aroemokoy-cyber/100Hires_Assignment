**Content/Summary/Key Takeaways**:

We've been generating AI message drafts for months. Daniel Malone edits most of them before they are sent, and until recently, we only saved the final version.

We were binning the most useful thing in the whole loop: the edit itself.

The original draft, the sent version, and the diff between them - that's where you see what the model gets wrong. The tone Dan fixes. The phrases he cuts every single time.

The fix was one database table. Original, edited, diff, a row on every save. Let it run for a month.

Then the feedback stops being a vague "the AI is bad at follow-ups" and becomes "the model wrote 'I hope this helps' 23 times last month and Dan cut it every time." The second version you can turn into a prompt change this week.

The features I'm proudest of here are the small captures like this one - the kind that quietly compound into a better model six months out. The gap between what the AI produces and what a human actually sends is the signal. Most teams let it evaporate.

What's your team generating right now that you're not storing?
