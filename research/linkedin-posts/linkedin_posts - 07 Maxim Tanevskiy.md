**Content/Summary/Key Takeaways**:

One click in Claude Code gives Anthropic your conversation for 5 years.

Most developers have never read this part of the fine print. I had not either.

Here is what is actually true, straight from Anthropic's own data-usage docs.

> The quick "How is Claude doing this session?" star rating is harmless. It stores your rating and nothing else. No transcript.

> The /feedback command and the thumbs up or down are a different animal.

Use them, and a full copy of your conversation, including your code, is uploaded to Anthropic. Stored for 5 years. It can be used to improve their products.

That is not the 30 days a non-training user assumes they have. It is 5 years, triggered by the act of giving feedback, not by your account settings.

> The opt-out you toggled controls the default. The feedback button is a separate door. Most people walk through it without reading the sign.

I am not telling anyone to drop Claude Code. I use it daily. It is the best in the game.

But if your sessions carry client code, NDAs, or anything you would not hand a stranger for 5 years, know what that button does first.

The real terms are never in the headline. They are in the part nobody reads.
